## section-context

type: text
label: Context
body: |
  Cars generate enormous amounts of data. Most drivers never see it.

  Drivelog explored a simple idea: what if your car could talk to you?

  Not through dashboards and error codes — but through clear insights about driving behaviour, vehicle health and everyday use.
media: context-img

## section-tension

type: text
label: Tension
body: |
  The technology existed. The data existed.

  What didn't exist was a coherent idea of what this thing was — product, app, brand, or service?

  Without that clarity, nothing could be designed. Nothing could be communicated. Nothing could be sold.

## section-decision

type: text
label: Decision
body: |
  One metaphor locked everything in place: the car talking to you.

  Not dashboards. Not diagnostics. A conversation.

  That single idea defined the product shape, the interface logic, the brand system, and the communication — all at once.
media: decision-img

## section-execution

type: text
label: Execution
body: |
  The OBD connector was designed as a physical counterpart to the app icon — a square with rounded corners. Half went into the car. The other half stayed as its shell.

  The speech bubble became the central visual element across every surface: product, interface, packaging, communication. One form. One idea. Total consistency.

  The mobile app translated vehicle health, driving behaviour, trip history, service reminders, and parking location into plain language — not for technicians, for drivers.

## section-outcome

type: text
label: Outcome
body: |
  Drivelog launched as a consumer-facing mobility brand inside one of the most complex technology ecosystems in Germany.

  It did this years before conversational vehicle interfaces became common.

  The metaphor held. The system shipped.
