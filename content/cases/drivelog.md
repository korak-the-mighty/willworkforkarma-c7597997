## section-context

type: text
label: Context
body: Cars generate enormous amounts of data. Most drivers never see it.\n\nDrivelog explored a simple idea: what if your car could talk to you?\n\nNot through dashboards and error codes — but through clear insights about driving behaviour, vehicle health and everyday use.
media: context-img

## section-tension

type: text
label: Tension
body: The technology existed. The data existed.\n\nWhat didn't exist was a coherent idea of what this thing was — product, app, brand, or service?\n\nWithout that clarity, nothing could be designed. Nothing could be communicated. Nothing could be sold.

## section-decision

type: text
label: Decision
body: One metaphor locked everything in place: the car talking to you.\n\nNot dashboards. Not diagnostics. A conversation.\n\nThat single idea defined the product shape, the interface logic, the brand system, and the communication — all at once.
media: decision-img

## section-execution

type: text
label: Execution
body: The OBD connector was designed as a physical counterpart to the app icon — a square with rounded corners. Half went into the car. The other half stayed as its shell.\n\nThe speech bubble became the central visual element across every surface: product, interface, packaging, communication. One form. One idea. Total consistency.\n\nThe mobile app translated vehicle health, driving behaviour, trip history, service reminders, and parking location into plain language — not for technicians, for drivers.

## section-outcome

type: text
label: Outcome
body: Drivelog launched as a consumer-facing mobility brand inside one of the most complex technology ecosystems in Germany.\n\nIt did this years before conversational vehicle interfaces became common.\n\nThe metaphor held. The system shipped.
