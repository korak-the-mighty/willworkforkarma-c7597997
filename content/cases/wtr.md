## section-context
type: text-media
mediaPosition: right
label: Context
video: context-img
Wörner Traxler Richter are one of Europe's leading architecture practices.
For decades they have designed some of the region's most complex healthcare, research and public buildings.
Their digital presence no longer reflected the quality, clarity and ambition of the practice behind it.
---
## section-tension
type: text-media
mediaPosition: left
label: Tension
image: tension-img
Architecture practices rarely believe in websites.
Their work lives in buildings, competitions and printed documentation.
Digital presence is often treated as a necessity rather than an opportunity.
The challenge was not to modernize the practice.
It was to earn its trust.
---
## section-decision
type: text
label: Decision
Before any design decision, the practice had to be understood.
How architects present work. How information is structured. How projects are evaluated. What actually matters in a profession built on precision and trust.
Only then could a digital identity emerge that felt true to the practice itself.
---
## section-execution
type: text
label: Execution
statement: A modular platform built around the work, not around the medium.
A new digital identity shaped through typography, color, interaction and motion.
Every transition, navigation pattern and interaction refined to feel effortless and precise.
Not a redesign for the sake of novelty.
A system that allowed the architecture to remain the focus while giving the practice a clear and scalable digital foundation.
