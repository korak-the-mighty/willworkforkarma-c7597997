# Cover Letter Workflow

This document is not a collection of templates.

It is a writing system.

Every new application follows the same process.

---

## Step 1 — Understand the Opportunity

Before writing anything:

* Read the job description carefully.
* Research the company, product and culture.
* Look beyond the listed requirements and ask what problem they are really trying to solve.
* Identify why they are hiring now and what success in the role would actually look like.

The goal is not to understand the job posting.

The goal is to understand what the company genuinely needs.


---

## Step 2 — Build the Case

Before writing a single sentence, answer these questions:

* What problem is this company actually trying to solve?
* Which of Henrik's beliefs naturally connects to that problem?
* Which experiences best support that belief?
* What should be left out because it isn't relevant?
* What is the one thing I want them to remember after reading this letter?

Only when the argument is clear should the writing begin.


---

## Step 3 — Choose One Core Belief

Every letter should revolve around a single central idea.

Examples:

* Most teams don't need more ideas. They need clarity.
* Strong ideas need ownership.
* Without finding the core, creativity becomes just a shell.
* Strong leadership starts with listening.

Do not repeat the same belief in multiple paragraphs.

One belief.
One story.

---

## Step 4 — Build the Argument

The structure should remain consistent.

Opening
100% custom

Body
Built from the relevant blocks in this document.

Closing
100% custom

The middle should become increasingly stable over time.
The opening and closing should always feel written specifically for that company.

---

## Step 5 — Support Beliefs with Proof

Philosophy earns attention.

Experience earns trust.

Lead with beliefs.

Support them with concrete examples from Henrik's career.

Do not explain the philosophy repeatedly.

Show it through experience.

---

## Step 6 — Henrik Test

Before sending, read the letter aloud.

Ask:

Would Henrik actually say this?

Does it sound honest?

Does it sound confident without becoming arrogant?

Does it sound human rather than corporate?

Remove every sentence that feels generic or AI-generated.

The goal is not to sound impressive.

The goal is to sound unmistakably like Henrik.

---

## Guiding Principle

Every cover letter should contain at least one sentence that only Henrik could have written.

If another Creative Director could copy the letter and it still feels authentic, the letter is not finished.

---

## Personalization

A personalized cover letter is not one that mentions the company repeatedly.

It is one that shows a genuine understanding of what the company needs and why Henrik's way of thinking is valuable in that specific context.

The words may only change by twenty percent.

The thinking behind them should change completely.

Ideas are easy.

Most of my career has been spent helping teams understand the problem before trying to solve it.

Once the direction becomes clear, execution tends to follow.

Someone still needs to decide what matters.

I don't believe creativity is a solo sport.

The strongest work usually comes from people with different perspectives pushing an idea forward together.

My job is to create the conditions for that to happen.

Clear direction does not kill creativity.

It gives creativity something to push against.
